DIRS: Single Image Reflection Separation via Deep Feature Interaction

Mingcv, Author Two, Author Three

Paper Code

Abstract

Single image reflection separation remains a challenging ill-posed problem. Existing methods often struggle with complex scenes where the reflection and transmission layers are intricately intertwined. In this paper, we propose DIRS, a novel approach that leverages Deep Feature Interaction to effectively distinguish and separate the two layers. Our method introduces a cross-layer attention mechanism that allows the network to explicitly model the relationships between reflection and transmission features, leading to a more accurate separation. Extensive experiments on benchmark datasets demonstrate that DIRS significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, particularly in scenarios with strong reflections and detailed backgrounds.
